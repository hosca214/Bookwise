Three directions for the landing page

Each approach addresses the tax problem and clarity differently. Pick the one that feels most like Bookwise.

A

Problem-first hero

Headline: "Tax season shouldn't be a surprise."

Subhead: "Bookwise automatically sets aside your taxes every time you get paid — so you always know what you owe, and what's yours to keep."

Leads with the #1 pain point. Clear and concrete. Risk: might feel narrow if someone's not in tax season mindset.

B

Show what it does

Headline: "Track your income. Set aside your taxes. Know what to pay yourself."

Subhead: "Bookwise is the financial tool built for wellness practitioners — coaches, trainers, and bodyworkers — who want clarity without the accounting jargon."

Instantly explains the 3 core jobs the app does. No ambiguity. Risk: reads a bit list-like, less emotional.

C

Problem + solution in one shot ⭐ Recommended

Headline: "Finally know where your money goes — and exactly what to set aside for taxes."

Subhead: "Bookwise tracks your income and expenses, splits your money into tax, operations, and take-home, and tells you what to pay yourself. Built for coaches, trainers, and bodyworkers."

Pairs the problem (financial fog + tax stress) with the solution (buckets + pay yourself). The subhead explains what the app IS without using the word "bookkeeping."
